Loose Pattern

The image showcases an artwork titled "Loose Pattern" by David Flynn. It appears to be an abstract sculpture primarily constructed from wood and metal. The sculpture consists of two vertical, weathered wooden beams full of holes and notches, standing parallel to each other on a flat metal base. Between the beams, there are several metal rods with ball-shaped ends, arranged in a diagonal crisscross pattern, reminiscent of a loose or abstract weaving. This piece has a rustic aesthetic due to the aged wood and the patina on the metal, suggesting a combination of natural decay and industrial elements. The arrangement might imply a sort of controlled chaos or an organic pattern, thus the title "Loose Pattern." The work could be interpreted as exploring themes of structure and randomness, craftsmanship, or the relationship between nature and industry.

I live with creativity

I am a professional artist who uses blacksmithing as my primary medium to create artwork. For the past two decades, I have created custom iron and steel artwork for homes, individuals, and public art commissions in Tucson, Arizona. My passions in life are creating art and teaching.
